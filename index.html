Why this app exists

Made by a boxer, for boxers

I box. And for years my round timer was whichever free app annoyed me least. One quit the moment my screen locked. One buried the bell under my playlist. The worst one interrupted round eight with a fullscreen ad. So I built the timer I actually wanted: set your rounds, hit start, put the phone in your pocket, and trust the bell.

It isn't just for boxing, either. Rounds are rounds. Muay Thai, kickboxing, MMA, HIIT circuits, conditioning work, jump rope intervals. There's a dedicated HIIT tab, and every preset can be reworked into whatever your coach dreamed up. If your training has a clock, this runs it.

The stuff people ask us about first

  • Do I need an account? No. There is nothing to sign up for. Download it, pick your rounds, train.
  • What data do you collect? None. No analytics, no ad tracking, no identifiers. Your presets and history stay on your phone.
  • Will the gym TV show ads? No. The TV screen is the clock and only the clock. The single sponsor card lives on the phone's home screen and nowhere near your workout.
  • So how is it free? One sponsor backs the app with a small card on the home screen. That card is the entire business model, which is why nothing about you needs collecting.
